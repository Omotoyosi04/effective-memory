Charles Babbage

Father of the computer

Here's a timeline of Charles Babbage

  • 1791 - Born in London, United Kingdom.
  • 1810 - Passed out from 30-Student Holmwood Academy and attended Trinity College, Cambridge.
  • 1812 - He was unsatisfied with the teaching method taught in Trinity College so he transferred to Peterhouse, Cambridge.
  • 1814 - He received a degree without examination and got married to Georgiana Whitmore
  • 1822 - He introduced his invention, Difference Engine, to the Royal Mechanical Society.
  • 1827 - Lost his father and his wife
  • 1835 - He came up with a design of an improved engine that would be able to calculate almost every equation.
  • 1846 - Started working on another Difference Engine that used three times fewer parts than its predecessor
  • 1871 - He died at the age of 79 in London.
